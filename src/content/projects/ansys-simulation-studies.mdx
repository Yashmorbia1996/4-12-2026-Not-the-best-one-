## Overview

These studies represent supporting engineering work where simulation was used to answer design questions earlier, before committing to expensive or slow hardware iteration.

## Situation / Problem

Thermal and structural decisions often move faster when there is a credible first-pass model that helps narrow the design space before fabrication or tooling changes.

## Constraints

- Early assumptions had to be made with incomplete information.
- The goal was directional insight, not false precision.
- Results still had to map back to practical design choices.

## My Role

I used simulation as a decision-support tool to test likely failure points, compare alternatives, and build stronger engineering intuition around the design.

## Actions / Approach

- Set up studies to understand heat flow, deflection, and sensitivity to design changes.
- Compared alternative concepts to identify likely performance tradeoffs.
- Used the results to inform where physical iteration would be most valuable.

## Skills Applied

- ANSYS
- Thermal reasoning
- Structural analysis
- Engineering tradeoff evaluation
- Pre-build decision support

## Validation / Testing

Simulation outputs were treated as one part of the engineering picture and intended to guide physical follow-up rather than replace testing.

## Outcome / Results

The work improved early design decision quality and reduced uncertainty before physical prototypes were built.

## Lessons Learned

Simulation is most valuable when it informs the next design move clearly. Even lightweight studies can create better decisions when assumptions are explicit and the results are tied back to practical hardware choices.
