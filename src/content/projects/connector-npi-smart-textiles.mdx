## Overview

This work sits in the supporting-project category rather than a full flagship case study, but it shows early exposure to product development in a real engineering environment. The scope blended connector-focused design work, smart-textile integration questions, and participation in cross-functional reviews.

## Situation / Problem

The team needed clearer mechanical direction for connector integration and more structured design thinking around manufacturability, verification, and design risk before committing to downstream builds.

## Constraints

- Requirements were still evolving.
- Multiple teams had a stake in the design direction.
- The work had to stay practical for manufacturing and reviewable by others.

## My Role

I supported CAD development, prototype-oriented iteration, DFM/DFA feedback, and engineering review preparation while learning how product decisions connect to manufacturing reality.

## Actions / Approach

- Built and refined SolidWorks concepts to evaluate connector packaging and interface behavior.
- Contributed to design reviews with a focus on manufacturability and assembly practicality.
- Supported verification-style thinking by helping frame what needed to be checked before more confidence could be placed in the design.

## Skills Applied

- SolidWorks
- DFM/DFA
- Connector integration
- Cross-functional communication
- Early verification planning

## Validation / Testing

Validation here was lighter than in a production-release case study. The value came from early iteration, design review feedback, and using engineering judgment to reduce ambiguity before later-stage development work.

## Outcome / Results

The project helped move concept work forward with better design clarity, stronger review conversations, and more manufacturing-aware decision-making.

## Lessons Learned

This work reinforced how useful early DFM thinking is, especially when requirements are still moving and cross-functional teams need a shared technical language.
