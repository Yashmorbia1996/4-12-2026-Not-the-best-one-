## Overview

These projects are a lighter portfolio layer that shows self-directed CAD practice. Rather than one single program, this page captures a collection of independent mechanical builds used to sharpen modeling discipline and design communication.

## Situation / Problem

Outside day-to-day work, I wanted a way to keep building design fluency through assemblies, mechanism studies, and manufacturable part concepts that forced clean modeling choices.

## Constraints

- No formal product team or downstream manufacturing partner
- Limited scope and time per project
- The work still needed to stay technically grounded rather than purely visual

## My Role

I defined the concepts, built the models, reviewed the assemblies, and used the work as a personal training ground for better CAD judgment.

## Actions / Approach

- Created part and assembly models with cleaner feature structure and more intentional references.
- Practiced mechanism studies to understand motion, interference, and packaging tradeoffs.
- Framed each concept with manufacturability in mind rather than treating the work as styling-only CAD.

## Skills Applied

- SolidWorks part modeling
- Assembly structure
- Mechanism layout
- DFM thinking
- Drawing clarity

## Validation / Testing

Validation centered on fit logic, assembly behavior, and internal consistency of the model structure rather than full product qualification.

## Outcome / Results

The collection improved speed, confidence, and discipline in CAD execution while adding visible breadth to the portfolio.

## Lessons Learned

Personal projects are most valuable when they are treated as engineering exercises, not just render exercises. The best learning came from decisions about constraints, packaging, and manufacturability.
