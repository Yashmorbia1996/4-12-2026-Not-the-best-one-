## Overview

This archive collects earlier engineering efforts that are useful for showing range, experimentation, and technical curiosity. These are not the headline case studies, but they help round out the portfolio story.

## Situation / Problem

Smaller builds and exploratory projects often disappear from a portfolio even though they show how engineering judgment and curiosity developed over time.

## Constraints

- Work from different stages and contexts
- Uneven depth across projects
- Lower need for full case-study treatment

## My Role

I selected and framed these projects as supporting evidence of how my engineering interests and execution habits developed before the more mature professional work.

## Actions / Approach

- Curated earlier projects into a lighter archive instead of forcing all of them into full case studies.
- Highlighted the engineering themes that still matter: curiosity, experimentation, and learning through builds.
- Kept the presentation concise so the archive supports the portfolio without overwhelming it.

## Skills Applied

- Concept exploration
- Mechanical curiosity
- Self-directed learning
- Early design iteration
- Technical framing

## Validation / Testing

Validation varied by project. The primary value here is not one formal result set, but the accumulation of experimentation and engineering range.

## Outcome / Results

The archive adds credible breadth and shows that the current portfolio is built on a longer arc of hands-on engineering exploration.

## Lessons Learned

Not every project needs the same depth. Sometimes the right move is to show range clearly and concisely, then let the strongest work carry the deeper case-study narrative.
