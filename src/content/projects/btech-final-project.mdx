## Overview

The B.Tech final project was an early foundation piece in learning how to move from an engineering idea to a more complete technical deliverable with documentation and presentation discipline.

## Situation / Problem

The project required turning an academic problem statement into a defined system, selecting a workable design direction, and presenting the engineering logic clearly.

## Constraints

- Limited resources compared with an industry environment
- Academic timelines and deliverables
- A need to balance technical work with clear communication

## My Role

I contributed to concept definition, mechanical design development, technical documentation, and final presentation of the work.

## Actions / Approach

- Broke the project into smaller engineering decisions instead of treating it as one large deliverable.
- Developed the design with attention to function, feasibility, and communication.
- Organized the work into a final package that could be explained clearly to reviewers.

## Skills Applied

- Mechanical design
- System thinking
- Documentation
- Technical presentation
- Structured problem solving

## Validation / Testing

The validation was aligned to academic project requirements and focused on demonstrating a reasoned engineering approach and a credible final solution.

## Outcome / Results

The project became an important early milestone in learning how to structure engineering work and communicate it with more confidence.

## Lessons Learned

The capstone reinforced that good engineering is not only about building the answer, but also about defining the problem, structuring the path, and explaining the reasoning behind the final decision.
